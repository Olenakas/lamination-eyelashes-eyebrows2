Answers to popular questions

There is an opinion that lamination of eyelashes is not a safe procedure, and it should be carried out no more than once a year. To dispel this myth, it is necessary to understand that keratin is a physiological protein for the hair, thanks to which the eyelashes thicken and heal. In addition, with each subsequent eyelash lamination procedure, keratin tends to accumulate in the thickness of the hair, and therefore, with each subsequent procedure, the effect of lamination becomes more noticeable and long-lasting.

The recommended time interval between the first and second eyelash lamination is about one month, then the procedure can be repeated every two months.

The procedure should be postponed in such cases:

  • the presence of diseases of the eyes and eyelids of an inflammatory nature, conjunctivitis, barley;
  • allergy, manifested by lacrimation, a feeling of sand in the eye and other signs of damage to the organ of vision.

In addition, the procedure is not carried out if the client has intolerance to any component of the solution used.

It is undesirable to carry out lamination of eyelashes during pregnancy, especially in the first trimester.

In addition, after the lamination of the eyelashes, there will be no difficulties with putting on and removing contact lenses, because there are no restrictions after this manipulation, you can safely touch both the eyelids and the eyelashes, this will not cause any damage to the result.

The effect after lamination of eyelashes, as a rule, is long-term and retains its original appearance until the beginning of their physiological change.

After two weeks with the change of eyelashes, the result of lamination descends. When laminating eyelashes, the hairs are strengthened not only from the outside, but also from the inside. Botulinum toxin, nourishing the hair follicles, prolongs their life cycle, and therefore the change of eyelashes is slower, which means that the effect after the procedure is longer.
